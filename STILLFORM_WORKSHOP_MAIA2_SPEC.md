**ARA Embers LLC · spec locked May 20, 2026 · status: SPEC LOCKED, build pending**

Workshop instrument #5, adapted from the MAIA-2 (Mehling et al. 2018). Capacities surface — **Sense** (interoception). Follows the capacity result-presentation pattern (SRIS, #3). Already cited in the Science Sheet.

---

## 1 · WHAT THIS IS

The interoception tool of the Workshop — the **Sense** layer, the foundation the whole precision loop runs on. You regulate and reframe what you can first feel; MAIA-2 measures how well a person senses, attends to, interprets, and trusts their internal signals. Companion to the bio-filter (the bio-filter reads current state; MAIA-2 reads the trait capacity to do that reading). Capacities-surface instrument; feeds the growth mirror, not a chip.

---

## 2 · SOURCE RESEARCH

**Primary citation:** Mehling, W. E., et al. (2018). The Multidimensional Assessment of Interoceptive Awareness, Version 2 (MAIA-2). *PLOS One*, 13(12). Revised from the 2012 MAIA.

**Structure:** 37 items, 6-point frequency (0 never – 5 always); higher = more interoceptive awareness for most subscales. Eight subscales: Noticing · Not-Distracting · Not-Worrying · Attention Regulation · Emotional Awareness · Self-Regulation · Body Listening · Trusting. Internal consistency α .64–.83. Validated cross-culturally (Dutch, Chinese, 26+ translations). Not a diagnostic tool. **Use the Brief MAIA-2 (24-item, 3 highest-loading items per subscale; Mehling) as the adaptation base** — 37 is too long for a Workshop take.

**Verbatim MAIA-2 items are NOT reproduced.** Original Stillform-voiced adaptations, faithful to each subscale.

---

## 3 · ADAPTATION PRINCIPLES

Holds USER TREATMENT (FRAMING_LAW v1.4 / CANON v1.9) and capacity-surface discipline (low = room to grow; high = usable foundation). Plus two MAIA-2-specific guards:

- **Not-Distracting and Not-Worrying are NOT simply higher-is-better** (MAIA-2 itself measures "both adaptive and maladaptive dimensions"). Extreme Not-Distracting can shade into hypervigilant body-monitoring; Not-Worrying is specifically about not *catastrophizing* sensations (which is adaptive). Frame these two as the adaptive construct — staying with a sensation without either suppressing it or spiraling about it — not as "more = better."
- **Trauma-sensitivity (critical for the body layer).** Interoceptive attention can be activating for trauma histories. The intro and pacing carry the locked gentleness; the take never pushes deeper body-attention than the user offers. This is the "design every surface as if the user is about to share something vulnerable" principle, applied literally.

## 4 · THE 24 ADAPTED ITEMS (Brief MAIA-2 base, 3 per subscale)

First-person, plain; interleaved in production. **Response shape:** four-point capacity self-report — *Rarely true · Sometimes · Often · Almost always true.*

### Workshop intro
> This looks at how you sense what's happening inside your body — the signals underneath thoughts and feelings, and how you relate to them. There's no right amount; this is the ground floor the rest of the practice is built on. If any of it feels like too much to look at right now, you can stop. About 4 minutes.

### Noticing (awareness of body sensations)
1. "I notice when something shifts in my body."
2. "I'm aware of comfortable sensations, not just uncomfortable ones."
3. "I pick up on subtle signals — a change in my breath, my chest, my stomach."

### Not-Distracting (not pushing discomfort away — adaptive framing)
4. "When something's uncomfortable in my body, I can stay with it rather than instantly distract from it."
5. "I don't always need to drown out a physical discomfort with something else."
6. "I can let an unpleasant sensation be there without rushing to escape it."

### Not-Worrying (discomfort without catastrophizing — adaptive framing)
7. "I can feel discomfort without it turning into worry about what it means."
8. "A physical sensation being unpleasant doesn't automatically alarm me."
9. "I can stay steady when my body feels off, without spiraling about it."

### Attention Regulation (directing and sustaining body attention)
10. "I can keep my attention on a body sensation when I choose to."
11. "I can bring my focus back to my body after it drifts."
12. "I can hold steady attention on my breath."

### Emotional Awareness (body–emotion link)
13. "I notice how my emotions show up in my body."
14. "I can feel where a feeling lives physically."
15. "When my mood shifts, I notice the body change that comes with it."

### Self-Regulation (using body attention to settle)
16. "I can calm myself by paying attention to my body."
17. "When I'm worked up, turning to my breath or body helps me settle."
18. "I use body awareness to bring myself back to steady."

### Body Listening (listening to the body for insight)
19. "I listen to my body for information about what's going on with me."
20. "My body tells me something useful if I pay attention."
21. "I check in with my body to understand how I'm doing."

### Trusting (experiencing the body as safe/trustworthy)
22. "I feel at home in my body."
23. "I trust my body's signals."
24. "My body feels like a safe place to be."

## 5 · RESULT PRESENTATION (capacity pattern — Sense)

Per the capacity template, by subscale, no scores:

- **Where each capacity sits + it's trainable + the practice builds it.** Low on Noticing/Attention/Body Listening/Trusting → "This is the most direct place the practice pays off — every breath and body scan trains it." High → "This sensing is already there to build the rest on."
- **Not-Distracting / Not-Worrying** read with their nuance: framed as "can you stay with a sensation without suppressing or spiraling," not "more is better."
- **Trusting** handled with extra care — low Trusting (the body doesn't feel safe) is never framed as a failing; it's named gently as a place the slow body work tends to help, with no pressure.

**Feeds the capacities growth mirror** (not a chip). Interoception is the most directly trainable capacity in the set — the bio-filter and body work move it — so it's a strong before/after marker. Internal precision read (not shown): better interoception = a cleaner sensory channel feeding the predictive model; the precision loop can't recalibrate on signals it can't sense.

---

## 6 · THRESHOLD + ACCESS

- **Push:** proposed when bio-filter use shows the user struggling to read their own state (frequent "all clear" alongside high activation language, or frequent "unsure"-type signals). Offers the look at the sensing skill underneath.
- **Pull:** Library → Workshop. Natural pairing with the body work.
- **Retake:** growth mirror; interoception shifts measurably with practice, so the retake tends to show real movement.

## 7 · FREQUENCY
~4 minutes (24 items, Brief MAIA-2 base).

## 8 · ADAPTATION CHALLENGES NOTED
- Brief MAIA-2 (24) over full (37) — confirmed base; watch take-length in testing.
- Not-Distracting/Not-Worrying adaptive framing must hold (not higher-is-better).
- Trauma-sensitivity: intro escape hatch + gentle pacing are non-negotiable.
- Overlap with the bio-filter (state vs trait) — result must distinguish "your capacity to read your body" from "what your body's doing right now," so it doesn't feel like the same question twice.
