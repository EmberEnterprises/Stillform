## Data Processing Agreement

This Data Processing Agreement ("**DPA**") is entered into between:

**ARA Embers LLC**, a [Delaware] limited liability company with
principal address at [Address] ("**Processor**" or "**Stillform**"),
and

**[Customer Legal Name]**, a [Customer entity type] with principal
address at [Customer Address] ("**Controller**" or "**Customer**"),

(each a "**Party**" and together the "**Parties**").

This DPA is incorporated into and supplements the Subscription
Agreement or Order Form between the Parties under which the
Customer has purchased access to the Stillform service (the
"**Agreement**"). In the event of a conflict between this DPA and
the Agreement on any matter relating to Processing of Customer
Personal Data, this DPA controls.

### 1. Definitions

Capitalized terms not defined here have the meanings set out in
the GDPR or the Agreement, as applicable.

- "**Applicable Data Protection Law**" means all data
  protection and privacy laws applicable to the Processing of
  Customer Personal Data under this DPA, including, where
  applicable, the EU General Data Protection Regulation (GDPR),
  the UK GDPR, the California Consumer Privacy Act (CCPA), and
  successor or analogous legislation.
- "**Customer Personal Data**" means Personal Data that
  Processor Processes on behalf of Controller in providing the
  Services, as further described in Annex A.
- "**Data Subject**" means an identified or identifiable
  natural person whose Personal Data is included in Customer
  Personal Data.
- "**Personal Data**", "**Processing**", "**Controller**",
  "**Processor**", and "**Sub-Processor**" each have the
  meaning given in the GDPR.
- "**Security Incident**" means a breach of security leading
  to the accidental or unlawful destruction, loss, alteration,
  unauthorized disclosure of, or access to Customer Personal
  Data.
- "**Services**" means the Stillform software-as-a-service
  product as described in the Agreement.

### 2. Roles and Scope

2.1 The Customer is the **Controller** and Stillform is the
**Processor** of Customer Personal Data described in Annex A.
Stillform Processes such Customer Personal Data only in
accordance with the Customer's documented instructions, the
Agreement, this DPA, and Applicable Data Protection Law.

2.2 **Members' Practice Content.** For the avoidance of doubt,
Stillform Processes practice content generated by individual
Members of the Customer's organization (sessions, journal
entries, reframes, biometric data, and similar) as an
independent Controller with respect to those Members. The
Customer does not control, direct, or have access to that
practice content; the Customer's role is limited to seat
provisioning, billing, and Admin-surface management as
described in the Stillform B2B Privacy Policy Addendum.

2.3 Customer is responsible for:

(a) ensuring that it has all necessary rights, consents, and
notices in place to enable the lawful transfer of Customer
Personal Data to Stillform for the duration and purposes
described in this DPA; and

(b) instructing Stillform only to Process Customer Personal Data
in a manner consistent with Applicable Data Protection Law.

### 3. Processor Obligations

Stillform shall:

(a) Process Customer Personal Data only on documented
instructions from the Customer, including with regard to
transfers of Customer Personal Data to a third country or
international organization, unless required to do so by
applicable law;

(b) ensure that personnel authorized to Process Customer Personal
Data are under appropriate confidentiality obligations;

(c) implement and maintain appropriate technical and
organizational measures to ensure a level of security
appropriate to the risk (see Annex B);

(d) assist the Customer, by appropriate technical and
organizational measures, insofar as this is possible, in
fulfilling the Customer's obligation to respond to requests
from Data Subjects under Applicable Data Protection Law;

(e) assist the Customer in ensuring compliance with its
obligations relating to security, breach notification, data
protection impact assessments, and prior consultation with
supervisory authorities, taking into account the nature of
the Processing and the information available to Stillform;

(f) at the choice of the Customer, delete or return all Customer
Personal Data to the Customer after the end of the provision
of the Services, unless retention of the Personal Data is
required by applicable law; and

(g) make available to the Customer all information necessary to
demonstrate compliance with the obligations laid down in
this DPA and allow for and contribute to audits, as set out
in Section 7.

### 4. Sub-Processors

4.1 The Customer authorizes Stillform's use of the
Sub-Processors listed in Annex C to Process Customer Personal
Data on the Customer's behalf.

4.2 Stillform shall:

(a) impose data protection obligations on each Sub-Processor
that are no less protective than those set out in this DPA;
and

(b) remain liable to the Customer for the performance of each
Sub-Processor's obligations.

4.3 Stillform will notify the Customer of any intended addition
or replacement of Sub-Processors by email to the billing
contact at least 30 days in advance. The Customer may object
to a new Sub-Processor on reasonable grounds relating to data
protection by giving written notice within that period. If the
Customer reasonably objects and the Parties cannot agree on a
resolution, the Customer may terminate the Agreement for
material breach without further liability.

### 5. International Transfers

Where the transfer of Customer Personal Data to a country
outside the European Economic Area, the United Kingdom, or
another adequate jurisdiction is required for Stillform to
provide the Services, the Parties shall implement appropriate
safeguards as required under Applicable Data Protection Law,
including, where applicable, the Standard Contractual Clauses
(EU 2021/914) and the UK International Data Transfer Addendum.

### 6. Security Incident Notification

6.1 Stillform shall notify the Customer of a Security Incident
involving Customer Personal Data without undue delay, and in
any event within **72 hours** of becoming aware of the incident.

6.2 The notification shall include, to the extent reasonably
available at the time:

(a) the nature of the Security Incident, including the
categories and approximate number of Data Subjects and
records concerned;

(b) the likely consequences of the Security Incident;

(c) the measures taken or proposed to be taken to address the
Security Incident and mitigate its possible adverse effects;
and

(d) the contact point from whom further information may be
obtained.

### 7. Audit Rights

7.1 Stillform shall make available to the Customer all
information reasonably necessary to demonstrate compliance with
this DPA, including a copy of Stillform's then-current SOC 2
report (when available; Stillform's SOC 2 Type 1 readiness
program is documented at `SOC2_TYPE_1_READINESS.md` in the
Stillform repository).

7.2 The Customer may, no more than once per year and upon at
least 30 days' written notice, conduct an audit of Stillform's
Processing of Customer Personal Data, subject to reasonable
confidentiality obligations and at the Customer's expense. The
audit shall not unreasonably interfere with Stillform's
operations. The Parties agree that a current SOC 2 report
satisfies the Customer's audit right unless the Customer can
demonstrate a specific concern not addressed by the report.

### 8. Data Subject Rights

8.1 If Stillform receives a request from a Data Subject
exercising rights under Applicable Data Protection Law in
relation to Customer Personal Data, Stillform will:

(a) where the request relates to seat-administrative data
(e.g., the Data Subject's role, status, or membership
records), promptly forward the request to the Customer; and

(b) where the request relates to the Data Subject's individual
practice content, handle the request directly with the Data
Subject, as the Data Subject controls that content.

### 9. Deletion and Return of Customer Personal Data

9.1 Upon termination or expiration of the Agreement, Stillform
shall, at the Customer's written option:

(a) delete all Customer Personal Data Processed on the
Customer's behalf, including seat-administrative records,
member emails, and audit log entries; or

(b) return all Customer Personal Data to the Customer in a
commonly used, machine-readable format.

9.2 Stillform will complete deletion or return within 60 days of
the Customer's instruction. Backups containing Customer Personal
Data will be deleted in accordance with Stillform's backup
retention schedule (see Annex B), and during that interval will
not be accessed for any purpose other than disaster recovery.

9.3 For clarity, this Section does not apply to Members'
individual practice content, which is governed by Stillform's
separate relationship with the Member.

### 10. Liability

Each Party's liability under this DPA is subject to the
limitation-of-liability provisions of the Agreement.

### 11. Governing Law

This DPA is governed by the laws of [Governing-Law Jurisdiction],
without regard to its conflict-of-laws principles. The Parties
submit to the exclusive jurisdiction of the courts of
[Governing-Law Jurisdiction] for any dispute arising out of or
in connection with this DPA.

### 12. Order of Precedence

In the event of a conflict between the terms of this DPA, the
Agreement, and any Annex, the order of precedence is: this DPA,
then the Annexes, then the Agreement.

## Annex A — Description of Processing

### Subject Matter
Provision of the Stillform service to the Customer's
organization, including seat administration and the
Member-facing application.

### Duration
The duration of the Agreement plus any retention periods
required by law or specified in Section 9.2.

### Nature and Purpose
Processing of Customer Personal Data for the purpose of:
- Provisioning and managing seats for Members
- Authenticating Members
- Recording and billing the Customer's subscription
- Generating an audit log of Admin actions
- Providing customer support to the Customer

### Categories of Data Subjects
- The Customer's designated Administrator(s)
- Members of the Customer's organization (employees,
  contractors, or others nominated by the Customer)

### Categories of Personal Data
- Names and email addresses of Administrators and Members
- Role and membership status
- Audit log entries (Admin actions, timestamps, IP addresses,
  user agents)
- Billing information (held by Lemon Squeezy as Sub-Processor)

For the avoidance of doubt, **Members' practice content** is
not Customer Personal Data within the meaning of this DPA;
Stillform Processes practice content as an independent
Controller with respect to the Member, as described in
Section 2.2.

### Special Categories of Personal Data
None Processed under this DPA. (Members' health-related practice
content is held under Stillform's separate Controller
relationship with the Member.)

## Annex B — Technical and Organizational Measures

### Encryption
- TLS 1.2+ for all data in transit
- AES-256 encryption at rest at the database layer
- AES-GCM device-local encryption for conversation history
  prior to cloud upload ⚠️ [PLANNED — NOT in the current build (June 2 2026): device-local encryption + cloud sync were features of the deleted old frontend; rebuild backlog. The TLS + database-layer claims above are current platform facts.]

### Access Control
- Service-role keys for backend data access, held only by
  Stillform's serverless functions and authorized personnel
- Row-Level Security (RLS) enforced for all user-facing
  database tables
- Multi-factor authentication required for administrative
  access to Sub-Processor dashboards

### Architectural Privacy Wall
The "privacy wall" between organization data and Member
practice content (see Section 2.2 and the Stillform B2B Privacy
Architecture document) is enforced at three layers: database
schema, application code, and audit logging. Schema-level
enforcement makes the bridge structurally absent.

### Audit Logging
Append-only audit log of all Admin actions taken on the
organization surface, including actor identity, action,
timestamp, IP address, and user agent.

### Backup Retention
Encrypted backups of database state retained for [retention
period] in line with disaster recovery requirements. Backups
are not used for any purpose other than recovery.

### Incident Response
Documented incident response plan with notification
obligations within 72 hours of awareness for Security Incidents
involving Customer Personal Data.

### Vendor Management
Sub-Processor list maintained and reviewed; data protection
obligations imposed on Sub-Processors no less protective than
those in this DPA.

### Personnel
Personnel authorized to access production systems are bound by
written confidentiality obligations and undergo background
checks consistent with the sensitivity of the data they may
access.
