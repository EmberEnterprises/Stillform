# STILLFORM ROUND 4 CONSULTATION — APRIL 30, 2026

## What this is

Three prior rounds of consultation produced: umbrella renames (Round 1), surface copy fixes (Round 2), and architectural shifts that proposed removing things the founder had already built (Round 3). All three failed to produce the spark she needs.

The pattern across all three failures: **the prompts gave the AIs permission to propose without giving them the constraints that would force them to think inside Stillform's actual reality.** They optimized for sounding clever instead of being right.

Round 4 fixes this by:

1. **Locking the non-negotiables explicitly** so the AIs can't propose removing what's load-bearing
2. **Naming the design tension the answer has to live inside** — prestige + real work, both at once
3. **Forcing the AIs to ground their answer in comparable products at the right altitude** — not theoretical proposals but lessons from products that already do what Stillform is trying to do

## THE PROMPT (paste this into each AI)

```
I'm consulting you on a product called Stillform. I've consulted four AIs three times already. Round 1 returned umbrella renames. Round 2 returned copy fixes. Round 3 returned architectural proposals to remove things the product already has. None of these landed because each round let me (the consultant) propose without grounding the proposal in Stillform's actual constraints.

I'm asking again with a tighter frame.

WHAT STILLFORM IS

Stillform is composure architecture. Composure is the visible silhouette of self-mastery practiced under load. Self-mastery is the through-line; composure is the felt outcome. The product is for people who carry intensity — founders, ND people, parents under load, people in recovery, anyone whose internal weather affects their external function.

The science is rigorous and citation-grounded throughout: Russell 1980 (chip framework), Lieberman 2007 (affect labeling), Schiller 2010 (memory reconsolidation), Wells 2009 (MCT), Balban 2023 (cyclic sighing), Barrett (constructed emotion), and many more. The science isn't decoration; it's structural.

The product targets a real felt-need: people whose intensity is internal weather, not external ambition. They've outgrown wellness app aesthetics ("you got this!") and self-help bro culture ("master your mindset"). They want a tool that respects them.

NON-NEGOTIABLES — DO NOT PROPOSE REMOVING THESE

The founder built these deliberately. Each survives a falsification test (taking it out would break something load-bearing in the science or the product's integrity). If your proposal requires removing any of these, the proposal is wrong:

1. **Calibration.** Initial setup produces regulation type (body-first / thought-first), signal profile (where intensity activates first in the body), and bias profile (cognitive distortions to watch for). This is implementation-intention formation per the science. The home screen's primary CTA is conditional on regulation type. Removing calibration removes the personalization that makes first-use trust real.

2. **Processing types as routing.** Body-first vs. thought-first determines which tool the user is led to first. State (chip + bio-filter) overrides type when needed. This isn't decoration — it's the product's core routing logic, grounded in the recognition that some people regulate via body and some via cognition.

3. **Morning check-in and End-of-Day rituals.** These are anchors that produce daily return. Morning is anticipatory calibration / inoculation; EOD is reflective consolidation. They serve different scientific functions. Weaving them into one ambient surface destroys both — and creates the failure mode where someone who hit EOD at 9pm has trouble returning at 11pm when something goes wrong.

4. **Differentiated tools (Reframe, Body Scan, Breathe, Self Mode).** Each is a different scientific intervention with a different job. They're not redundant widgets in a toolbox. The user can stay productively unaware of them by default — the system routes — but the underlying differentiation must remain intelligible to anyone who wants to understand what the system did.

5. **Sessions as discrete units.** A user opens the app, does the work, closes the app. The product's own truth: "the less you need the app, the more it's working." Stillform must NOT become an always-on ambient layer or a self-monitoring infrastructure. That contradicts the design philosophy.

6. **The data layer in My Progress as a destination, not a follower.** A user who wants to see their pattern goes there deliberately. The data does NOT chase the user with motivational pings. ("This is your third rep today" is gamification energy; it's noise the user doesn't want.) Data is evidence the user can return to when they want it.

THE TENSION YOU MUST DESIGN INSIDE

The founder articulated the integrity contract precisely:

"Prestige, innovative, different, makes an impact to the user with red carpet service but also makes them do real work. Self-mastery is not easy, it's a daily practice that helps you keep your composure and clarity which makes you feel sovereign and gives you grace and poise but the app needs the user to get there."

Two things at once:
- **Red carpet service** — the user feels respected, met, held by an architecture that doesn't waste their effort
- **Real work** — the practice demands honest engagement; the product doesn't pretend self-mastery is easy

Most products fail one or the other. Wellness apps deliver red-carpet aesthetics with no actual demand (the user closes feeling soothed but unchanged). High-performance apps deliver demand without service (the user closes feeling worked but not respected). Stillform's integrity claim requires both.

Most consultant feedback also fails this tension by reaching for one side:
- "Make it more accessible" → dilutes the work
- "Make it more rigorous" → loses the service
- "Make it more ambient" → both at once but at the cost of the user actually finishing anything

THE QUESTION

Inside the non-negotiables, with the prestige-plus-real-work tension as the design constraint:

What does Stillform's flow look like at its best?

Not what's missing. Not what to remove. Not what to rename. **What does the lived experience of using Stillform look like when it's reaching its full potential — given everything that must stay in place?**

To ground your answer, name 2-3 existing products (any category — software, physical product, service, ritual) that achieve BOTH red-carpet service AND demand real work. Tell me what they share. What's the shape of "service that demands"?

Then apply that shape to Stillform. Where in the current flow does Stillform deliver this shape, and where does it fall short of the shape it could achieve? Be specific about flow moments — entry, the work itself, transition between phases, exit, return on subsequent days.

CONSTRAINTS ON YOUR ANSWER

- 1500 words maximum.
- The 2-3 reference products are required. They're how you prove you understand the altitude. Without them, your answer is theoretical.
- "Service that demands" is the shape we're trying to characterize. Help me see it.
- Do not propose removing any item from the non-negotiables list. If your answer would require removing one, the answer is wrong; find a different path.
- Specific over abstract. "When the user opens the app on day 8 of practice, the welcome state should..." beats "the app should feel more aligned with the user's journey."
- If you genuinely think Stillform's current flow already achieves the shape — say so, name where, and tell the founder what she's missing about her own product. That answer is allowed.

WHAT GOOD LOOKS LIKE

Examples of altitude (these are illustrative, not necessarily right):

- "Peloton's Bike achieves service-that-demands by treating every ride as a one-on-one with a real instructor who knows your name and your history. The instructor is the demand; the production is the service. Stillform's analog: the AI Reframe should know the user has done this work before, name what they've practiced, and meet them at the level they've reached — not start from zero each session."

- "Mast Brothers chocolate (when it was good) demanded that you taste origin and process — the user had to learn to taste. That demand was the service. Stillform's analog: the chip system asks the user to read their own state with precision. Most apps don't ask this; Stillform does. The 'service' in the demand is that the user learns granularity over time, which is itself the practice. The shape is right; the surfacing of that learning back to the user is where the gap lives."

- "Daily Stoic (the journal product) achieves service-that-demands by giving the user a single page per day with one prompt and one quote. The constraint is the service. Stillform's analog: maybe the entry surface should not present multiple choices but one named first move with quiet override available — not because the others are removed, but because choosing IS work, and that work doesn't belong at entry."

These are illustrative. Yours might point a different direction. The form to follow: real product → shape it shares → application to Stillform → specific flow moment.

THE FOUNDER'S ASK BEHIND THE ASK

She knows the bones are good. She's not looking for validation or for someone to call the architecture wrong. She's looking for the SHAPE that would make Stillform reach its potential without breaking what makes it Stillform. She's asking the question that produces a star — but she's earned a real answer, not another consultant's reflex move.

You have one shot to give her something she can actually use.
```

## After getting the four responses

The synthesis filter for Round 4:

1. **Did each AI actually use real reference products?** Throw out responses that skipped this — they didn't read the prompt.
2. **Does each proposal respect the non-negotiables?** Throw out any that don't.
3. **Does the proposal name the tension and design inside it?** Or did it pick one side (prestige OR work)?
4. **Is the proposal grounded in specific flow moments?** Or is it abstract architectural language?

Convergent claims (3+ AIs name the same shape) are load-bearing. Divergent claims that respect the non-negotiables are decisions for Arlin. Claims that propose removing non-negotiables are simply discarded.

## Why this prompt is built differently than Round 3

Round 3 asked "what would you change about the shape." That's an open invitation to remove things. The AIs took the invitation. Round 4 asks "what does the flow look like at its best, inside the constraints" — a much harder question because they have to design within reality, not propose ideal worlds.

The reference-products requirement is the load-bearing mechanism. It forces the AIs to ground their answer in something real. The shape of "service that demands" exists in the world; products achieve it; the AIs have to name those products and identify what they share. Then they apply that shape to Stillform. This converts theoretical proposals into grounded analysis.

The non-negotiables list is the second load-bearing mechanism. It removes the easy escapes. An AI can't propose ambient mode (violates #5). Can't propose zero-setup (violates #1). Can't propose tool dissolution (violates #4). What remains is the actual question: given all this stays, what's the shape?

If this prompt also fails to produce a spark, the consultation track is exhausted. The founder's instinct — that the next move is testing on phone and naming friction in her own words — is the right one. But this round is worth running because the question is finally tight enough to deserve a real answer.
