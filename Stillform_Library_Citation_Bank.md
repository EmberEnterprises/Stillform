## NEUROPLASTICITY / TRANSFER (the load-bearing chapter — and the I1 vindication)
- CLAIM: skills practiced in isolation do not reliably generalize to real life;
  this is why Stillform practices the move IN the real moment (field reps), not as
  a game that's assumed to transfer.
  - Gobet & Sala (2023), "Cognitive Training: A Field in Search of a Phenomenon,"
    Perspectives on Psychological Science 18(1):125–141. Far-transfer effect is
    essentially null across training types.
  - Sala, Aksayli, Tatlidil, et al. (2019), "Near and Far Transfer in Cognitive
    Training: A Second-Order Meta-Analysis," Collabra: Psychology 5(1):18. Near
    transfer occurs and is modulated by the trainee; far transfer does not.
  - HONEST CAVEAT + BRAND USE: name the industry's overclaim plainly (Lumosity/
    BrainHQ: no compelling far-transfer evidence). Stillform's honesty IS the
    differentiator — we teach a practice that migrates into life, not a game that
    promises to make you globally smarter.

## SLEEP (load-bearing: the capacity the practice builds consolidates in sleep)
- CLAIM: sleep reliably supports memory consolidation in healthy adults.
  - Motor-memory meta-analysis (Neurosci. Biobehav. Rev., 2020): sleep benefits
    consolidation of motor memories vs equivalent wake.
  - Ng, Noh, Spencer (2025), eLife, DOI 10.7554/eLife.101992: SO–spindle coupling
    predicts overnight retention (mechanism; effects modest — ~0.5% variance,
    state honestly).
  - CAVEAT: effects moderated by task type, test format (recall>recognition), and
    age; not a universal booster. State moderate, not miraculous.
- CLAIM: writing tomorrow's anchor at bedtime settles the mind toward sleep.
  - Scullin et al. (2018), JEP:General 147(1):139–146 [ALREADY HOUSED]. Single
    well-controlled polysomnography study; stated at true size in-app already.
