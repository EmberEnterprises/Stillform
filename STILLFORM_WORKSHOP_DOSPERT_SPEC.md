# STILLFORM WORKSHOP — INSTRUMENT #7: RISK PROFILE

**ARA Embers LLC · spec locked May 20, 2026 · status: SPEC LOCKED, build pending**

Workshop instrument #7, adapted from the DOSPERT (Weber, Blais & Betz 2002; Blais & Weber 2006). The **profile surface** — and the spec that establishes the **third and final result frame: value-neutral profile/mirror.** Lower priority than the pattern-work and capacities instruments; ships as the "know your shape" surface.

---

## 1 · WHAT THIS IS

The self-knowledge tool of the Workshop — pure mirror, nothing to fix. Where pattern-work surfaces what's running wrong and capacities mirror trainable skills, the profile surface shows you a *shape* with no better-or-worse to it. DOSPERT maps where you take risk and where you don't, across the domains of a life. Its one genuinely useful insight: **you are not "a risk-taker" or "risk-averse" — risk appetite is domain-specific.** Many people read themselves as cautious and discover they're cautious *financially* and bold *socially*, or the reverse. That's real self-knowledge, delivered with zero judgment.

---

## 2 · SOURCE RESEARCH

**Primary citation:** Weber, E. U., Blais, A.-R., & Betz, N. (2002); revised Blais & Weber (2006, 30-item). Cited 2000+ times.

**Structure:** risk-taking likelihood across five content domains — ethical, financial (investing + gambling), health/safety, recreational, social. (The full instrument also measures risk *perception* and *expected benefit*; Stillform's adaptation uses the risk-taking-likelihood read as the core.) 5- or 7-point Likert; subscale α ~.70–.86. **The key empirical finding:** within-person variation across domains is ~7× the between-person variation — risk-taking is *not* a stable global trait. This domain-specificity is the whole point of using it.

**Verbatim DOSPERT items are NOT reproduced.** Original Stillform-voiced adaptations, faithful to each domain (Stillform uses original everyday scenarios, not DOSPERT's specific activities).

---

## 3 · ADAPTATION PRINCIPLES — THE PROFILE FRAME (new, third result frame)

Holds USER TREATMENT (FRAMING_LAW v1.4 / CANON v1.9), and establishes the **value-neutral profile frame**:

- **No "right" level — nothing to fix.** Neither high nor low risk-taking is a problem, a capacity to grow, or a deficit. The result is a *self-portrait*, not a diagnosis or a skill gradient. This is distinct from both the dysfunction-pattern frame (pattern-work) and the capacity frame (capacities).
- **No scores, no prevalence** (as always). "Here's your shape," never a number or a comparison to others.
- **Self-knowledge IS the path forward.** The profile frame satisfies "never surface a problem without a path forward" trivially — there's no problem; the seeing is the value. (If anything actionable surfaces, it's the user's own "huh, I didn't realize I was so cautious there" — their move, not Stillform's prescription.)

---

## 4 · THE 15 ADAPTED ITEMS (3 per domain, Stillform-trimmed)

First-person likelihood; interleaved in production. **Response shape:** likelihood — *Very unlikely · Unlikely · Likely · Very likely* ("How likely are you to…").

### Workshop intro
> This one's just a mirror — no right answers, nothing to work on. It maps where you lean toward risk and where you hold back, across different parts of life. Most people find they're not simply "cautious" or "bold" — they're one thing here and the opposite there. About 3 minutes.

### Ethical
1. "Bend the truth a little to keep a situation smooth."
2. "Cut a corner you know you probably shouldn't."
3. "Take credit that wasn't fully yours when no one would notice."

### Financial
4. "Put a meaningful chunk of money into something risky for a bigger possible return."
5. "Make a bet where the odds are long but the payoff is large."
6. "Spend on something significant without a clear plan to cover it."

### Health / Safety
7. "Skip a precaution you know you're supposed to take."
8. "Do something physical that carries a real chance of getting hurt."
9. "Put off dealing with a health thing you know you should handle."

### Recreational
10. "Try an activity that scares you a little, just for the thrill."
11. "Go somewhere unfamiliar without a plan."
12. "Take on a physical challenge well outside your comfort zone."

### Social
13. "Share an unpopular opinion in a group that disagrees."
14. "Tell someone close a hard truth that might upset them."
15. "Put yourself out there socially where you might be rejected."

## 5 · RESULT PRESENTATION (the profile/mirror frame — third template)

No scores. The result is a **shape across the five domains**, with the domain-specificity insight front and center:

> Here's where you lean toward risk and where you hold back. [Domain-by-domain shape, plain.] Notice the spread — most people aren't simply cautious or bold; they're one way in one part of life and the opposite in another. That spread is the interesting part. Nothing here needs fixing — this is just a clearer picture of how you actually move.

- **Lead with the spread, not any single domain** — the cross-domain pattern is the insight.
- **No domain framed as good or bad.** High social-risk-taking isn't "brave," low health-risk-taking isn't "wise" — just *you*.
- **Optional, light precision note (only if it serves, never clinical):** a domain where you're notably risk-averse can be a place a worst-outcome read runs heavy — a thread the user can pull if they want, never a problem Stillform names for them.

**Feeds:** the profile surface (its own light surface), not a chip and not the capacities growth mirror. Lowest-stakes Workshop output — pure self-portrait. May be the most *shareable* result (a "composure shape" card territory), but that's downstream.

---

## 6 · THRESHOLD + ACCESS

- **Push:** rarely pushed — it's not a problem-surfacing tool. At most, offered as a light "want to see your shape?" option, not triggered by distress signals.
- **Pull:** Library → Workshop. The low-pressure, curiosity-driven take — a good one for users not ready for the heavier instruments.
- **Retake:** less of a growth mirror than the capacities instruments (risk shape is fairly stable); a retake is more "has anything shifted" than "have you grown."

## 7 · FREQUENCY
~3 minutes (15 items, Stillform-trimmed from 30).

## 8 · ADAPTATION CHALLENGES NOTED
- **The profile frame is new** — build must not let it inherit the pattern-work "here's a problem" tone or the capacities "here's a skill to grow" tone. It's a mirror; the discipline is *no prescription at all.*
- **Lowest priority of the seven** — peripheral to the core metacognition/composure mission. Confirm it earns a slot vs. being the most deferrable to channel-growth (though per "nothing is post-launch," it ships).
- **Ethical-domain items** need care — framed as honest self-report of tendency, never as a morality test.
- DOSPERT's risk-perception/expected-benefit dimensions are dropped for simplicity; revisit if the profile proves valuable enough to deepen.
