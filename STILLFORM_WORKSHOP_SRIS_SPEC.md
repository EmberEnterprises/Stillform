# STILLFORM WORKSHOP — INSTRUMENT #3: SELF-REFLECTION & INSIGHT

**ARA Embers LLC · spec locked May 20, 2026 · status: SPEC LOCKED, build pending**

Workshop instrument #3, adapted from the SRIS (Grant, Franklin & Langford 2002). First instrument in the **capacities surface** of Bias Profile (Phase 5 sub-item #4) — and therefore the spec that establishes the **capacity result-presentation pattern** (the way MCQ-30 established the dysfunction-pattern one). Per the May 20 architecture lock, the capacities surface mirrors the exact skills Stillform trains: SRIS = **See yourself** (metacognition), alongside MAIA-2 (Sense), ERQ (Settle), IRI (See others).

---

## 1 · WHAT THIS IS

The metacognition tool of the Workshop — and the most Stillform-core instrument in the catalog, because it measures the exact thing the product trains. Where the pattern-work instruments (MCQ-30, CD-Quest) surface *what's running wrong*, the capacities instruments mirror *the skills themselves* — where they sit now, and how they grow. SRIS measures self-awareness directly, split into the two halves that match Stillform's own framing: **Self-Reflection** (the *act* of examining your thinking) and **Insight** (the *clarity* that examination resolves into).

It is NOT a pattern to fix and does NOT produce a watch-list chip. It feeds the **capacities surface** — a before/during/after growth mirror of the user's own self-awareness. This is the first capacities instrument; its result pattern is the template for ERQ, MAIA-2, and IRI.

## 2 · SOURCE RESEARCH (integrity-through-research)

**Primary citation:** Grant, A. M., Franklin, J., & Langford, P. (2002). The Self-Reflection and Insight Scale: A new measure of private self-consciousness. *Social Behavior and Personality*, 30(8), 821–836.

**Structure of the source instrument:** 20 items, agreement scale, two factors — Self-Reflection (12 items; the engagement in and inclination toward examining one's thoughts, feelings, behavior) and Insight (8 items; the clarity of understanding of one's thoughts, feelings, behavior). Internal consistency high (SR α ≈ .91, Insight α ≈ .87). A short form exists. Critically, SRIS was built for **coaching and performance enhancement in non-clinical populations** as well as clinical — it is an *enhancement* instrument, which fits Stillform's audience exactly (enhancement-seekers, not patients).

**The construct framing that makes it core:** Grant frames self-reflection and insight as the metacognitive factors central to purposeful, directed change. This is Stillform's own thesis in the literature's words.

**The verbatim SRIS items are NOT reproduced here or anywhere in Stillform.** Stillform's items are original adaptations faithful to each factor's construct, in Stillform's voice. Fidelity is to the *construct*, not the wording.

## 3 · ADAPTATION PRINCIPLES (from USER TREATMENT framing law)

Holds the USER TREATMENT principles (FRAMING_LAW v1.4 / CANON v1.9):

- **Non-clinical voice.** Plain first-person items; the rigor is in the construct, the voice is Stillform's.
- **No scores shown.** No number, rank, or good/bad judgment. "Where this sits for you," never a value.
- **No prevalence framing.** Grounding comes from the model (Grant's self-reflection/insight construct), never distribution.
- **Capacity, not deficit (the defining principle of this surface).** Low does NOT read as deficit — it reads as room to grow, the most direct place the practice pays off. High does NOT read as a gold star — it reads as a usable foundation. Both honest, both pointing forward.
- **Honesty paired with acceptance + every result has a path forward** (here, the path is the practice itself — the thing that builds the capacity).

## 4 · THE 20 ADAPTED ITEMS

Grouped by factor for clarity; **in production, items interleave.** First-person, plain.

### Workshop intro (shown before the user starts)
> This is a look at the skill underneath everything else here: how you watch your own mind, and how clearly that watching pays off. Two halves — how much you turn things over, and how much that turning-over actually resolves into understanding. There's no good score; this is a starting line, not a grade. Mark where each sits for you. About 5 minutes.

### Response shape
A four-point capacity self-report (the locked capacities/frequency shape): **Rarely true · Sometimes · Often · Almost always true.**

### SR — Self-Reflection (the act of examining your own mind)
1. "I spend time looking at why I feel the way I do."
2. "When something affects me, I go back over it to understand what happened."
3. "I'm the kind of person who examines my own reactions."
4. "I notice myself thinking about my own thinking."
5. "I make a point of understanding my own behavior, not just acting."
6. "When I'm off, I try to work out what's underneath it."
7. "I'm curious about why I do what I do."
8. "I take time to reflect on my choices after I make them."
9. "I pay attention to the patterns in how I respond to things."
10. "I think about how my feelings connect to what I do."
11. "I question my own assumptions about myself."
12. "Examining myself is something I do on my own, not only when something's wrong."

### IN — Insight (the clarity that examination resolves into)
13. "I usually understand why I feel the way I do."
14. "I have a clear sense of what drives my behavior."
15. "When I reflect, I tend to actually arrive somewhere — not just circle."
16. "I can usually name what's going on inside me."
17. "My reasons for acting are clear to me most of the time."
18. "When I'm upset, I can usually figure out what it's really about."
19. "I tend to come away from thinking-it-over with more clarity, not less."
20. "I trust my read on my own inner state."

## 5 · RESULT PRESENTATION (the CAPACITY pattern — template for the capacities surface)

No scores. Reports *where each capacity sits* and what builds it. The two factors are read **together** — the relationship between them is the real signal.

### The four readings (from the SR × Insight relationship)
- **Reflection high + Insight high** → *generative self-awareness.* "You turn things over, and the turning-over lands somewhere. That's the engine of this whole practice already running — Stillform sharpens it." (Usable foundation, never a gold star.)
- **Reflection high + Insight low** → *the looping signal* (the most important reading). "You examine yourself a lot, but it doesn't always resolve — the reflection circles instead of clarifying. That's not a flaw; it's the exact gap the practice closes. Reflection that loops is rumination; reflection that lands is insight. Stillform's whole job is the conversion." Surfaced gently, never as deficit. This is the reading SRIS exists to catch.
- **Reflection low + Insight present** → "You don't spend much time examining your mind, but when you do it's clear. Room to grow is in the looking, not the understanding."
- **Reflection low + Insight low** → "This is a skill you haven't built much yet — which means it's the most direct place the practice pays off. Everything else here trains it." (Room to grow, never deficit.)

### What it feeds (NOT a chip — the capacities growth mirror)
SRIS does not produce a watch-list chip. It seeds the **capacities surface**: a baseline for self-awareness that the retake mirrors against over time. The reflection→insight relationship becomes a tracked growth line (no number — the shape of the change is the data). The AI's Reframe context can carry "this user reflects but tends to loop" as a steer toward insight-closing prompts.

### The internal precision read (not shown)
In precision terms: insight = a well-calibrated self-model; rumination = high-precision self-focused loops that never update. The reflection-high/insight-low reading is the behavioral signature of looping, and it steers the AI toward the prediction-error / clarity-closing moves of the Precision Framework. Internal only.

## 6 · THE REFLECTION→INSIGHT NUANCE (why this instrument is read as a relationship, not two scores)

The research is explicit: self-reflection on its own does not predict well-being — *insight* does (Harrington & Loffredo; Lyke 2009). More reflection is not automatically better; reflection that fails to resolve is rumination. This is the same generative-vs-looping distinction as MCQ-30's CSC subscale — but where CSC was a single nuanced subscale, here it is the *whole point* of the instrument. Stillform must never reward raw reflection as a high score; the conversion of reflection into insight is the skill, and the practice is the converter. The result presentation above is built entirely around the relationship, not the two factors in isolation.

---

## 7 · THRESHOLD + ACCESS (push and pull)

- **Push:** the AI proposes SRIS when a user shows the looping signature across Reframes — lots of self-examination language that circles without resolving (the in-vivo version of reflection-high/insight-low). The proposal offers the closer look at the skill itself.
- **Pull:** always available in the Library → Workshop section. As the flagship capacities instrument, it's a natural first Workshop take for a new user wanting a baseline.
- **Re-administration:** designed for retake — it's a growth mirror. Retake surfaces the shift in the reflection→insight relationship over time (no scores; the direction of change is the report).

## 8 · FREQUENCY

~5 minutes (20 items, single rating). Short form is the fallback if testing shows fatigue.

## 9 · ADAPTATION CHALLENGES NOTED (for future review)

- **The two factors must be read together, never as standalone scores.** The single biggest design risk is presenting "high reflection" as good in isolation — that rewards rumination. The result logic is relationship-first.
- **Capacity framing discipline.** Low must never read as deficit; high must never read as gold star. This is the new muscle the capacities surface requires, distinct from the pattern-work instruments.
- **Overlap with MCQ-30's CSC** (cognitive self-consciousness ≈ self-reflection). If a user takes both, cross-reference — CSC's generative-vs-looping reformulation and SRIS's reflection→insight reading are the same idea; don't double-name it.
- **No chip.** Build must not force SRIS into the chip/watch-list pattern; it feeds the capacities growth mirror, a different surface. (This is the structural template the other capacities instruments follow.)
