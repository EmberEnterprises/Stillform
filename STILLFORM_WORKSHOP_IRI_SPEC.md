# STILLFORM WORKSHOP — INSTRUMENT #6: INTERPERSONAL REACTIVITY (SEE OTHERS)

**ARA Embers LLC · spec locked May 20, 2026 · status: SPEC LOCKED, build pending**

Workshop instrument #6, adapted from the IRI (Davis 1980). Capacities surface — **See others**. Completes the capacities loop: Sense (MAIA-2) → Settle (ERQ) → See yourself (SRIS) → **See others** (IRI), which is Stillform's full philosophy: "regulate yourself → see yourself clearly → see others clearly → let them in." Demonstrates that result frames are **per-subscale, not per-instrument.**

---

## 1 · WHAT THIS IS

The social-reading tool of the Workshop — the **See others** layer. Stillform's arc doesn't end at self-awareness; it opens outward (see others clearly → let them in). IRI measures the components of that outward read: how readily you take another's viewpoint, how much you feel with others, and — the nuanced part — whether feeling-with tips into being swamped. Capacities-surface instrument; feeds the growth mirror, not a chip. Its Perspective-Taking subscale bridges to the pattern-work strand as the adaptive inverse of the mind-reading distortion.

---

## 2 · SOURCE RESEARCH

**Primary citation:** Davis, M. H. (1980, 1983). The Interpersonal Reactivity Index. The most widely used multidimensional empathy measure; strong psychometrics.

**Structure:** 28 items, 5-point scale, four 7-item subscales — Perspective Taking (cognitively adopting others' viewpoint), Empathic Concern (other-oriented compassion), Fantasy (identifying with fictional characters), Personal Distress (self-oriented anxiety at others' distress). Two cognitive (PT, FS), two affective (EC, PD). A validated 16-item short form exists — **the short-form base is preferred** for Workshop length.

**Verbatim IRI items are NOT reproduced.** Original Stillform-voiced adaptations, faithful to each subscale.

---

## 3 · ADAPTATION PRINCIPLES

Holds USER TREATMENT (FRAMING_LAW v1.4 / CANON v1.9). **This instrument is the clearest case of per-subscale result frames** — the adaptation principle is that each subscale gets the frame its construct demands:
- **Perspective Taking, Empathic Concern → capacity frame** (trainable, more is generally adaptive; low = room to grow, high = foundation).
- **Personal Distress → nuanced/regulation-adjacent** (high is NOT "more empathy"; it's getting flooded, which impairs helping — links to the Settle layer).
- **Fantasy → value-neutral profile** (just a trait; no better/worse — a preview of DOSPERT's profile frame).

---

## 4 · THE 16 ADAPTED ITEMS (short-form base, 4 per subscale)

First-person, plain; interleaved in production. **Response shape:** four-point capacity self-report — *Rarely true · Sometimes · Often · Almost always true.*

### Workshop intro
> This looks at how you read and respond to other people — the outward half of seeing clearly. How easily you step into someone else's view, how much you feel with them, and whether that feeling stays steady or sweeps you up. No right answer; just where each sits for you. About 3 minutes.

### PT — Perspective Taking (adopting others' viewpoint)
1. "I try to see things from the other person's side before I land on a judgment."
2. "When someone disagrees with me, I try to picture how it looks from where they sit."
3. "I can usually understand why someone did what they did, even when I don't agree."
4. "Before I decide what someone meant, I consider that there might be more than one read."

### EC — Empathic Concern (other-oriented compassion)
5. "I feel for people who are having a hard time."
6. "Someone else's struggle moves me."
7. "When someone's hurting, I'm drawn to care rather than turn away."
8. "I notice and feel the weight of what others are going through."

### FS — Fantasy (identifying with characters)
9. "I get pulled into the feelings of characters in a story or film."
10. "I imagine myself in the place of people I'm reading about or watching."
11. "A good story can put me right inside someone else's experience."
12. "I get absorbed in the inner lives of characters."

### PD — Personal Distress (self-oriented overwhelm at others' distress)
13. "When someone near me is upset, I get rattled myself."
14. "Being in a tense emotional situation throws me off."
15. "Other people's strong emotions can swamp me."
16. "When things get emotionally intense around me, I struggle to stay steady."

## 5 · RESULT PRESENTATION (per-subscale frames — See others)

The defining feature: each subscale uses its own frame, no total.

- **Perspective Taking (capacity)** → "How readily you step into another viewpoint. This is the outward edge of the practice — and the opposite of filling in the why with no data. High: a real foundation for letting people in. Low: the most direct place the outward work grows." (Bridges explicitly to the *filling in the why* / mind-reading chip — they're inverses.)
- **Empathic Concern (capacity)** → named as a strength where present; where low, framed as room to grow, never coldness-as-deficit.
- **Personal Distress (nuanced, regulation-adjacent)** → "When others are in distress, you tend to get swept up rather than stay steady. That's not too much heart — it's the feeling-with outrunning your footing. The regulation work (Settle) is exactly what lets you stay *with* someone without drowning." Never framed as a flaw in caring.
- **Fantasy (value-neutral profile)** → "Just how you're wired toward stories and other minds — no better or worse. Noted, not graded." (Preview of the profile frame.)

**Feeds the capacities growth mirror** (not a chip). The PT line is a tracked growth marker for "see others clearly." Internal precision read (not shown): perspective-taking = actively not over-weighting your own viewpoint prior; high Personal Distress = others' affect over-weighting your own regulation — a precision/regulation interaction.

## 6 · THRESHOLD + ACCESS

- **Push:** proposed when Reframes show recurring social-read patterns — heavy mind-reading language (low PT signature) or getting swamped by others' states (high PD signature). Offers the look at the outward read.
- **Pull:** Library → Workshop. Pairs naturally with the SRIS "see yourself" take (self → others).
- **Retake:** growth mirror; PT and the PD-steadiness shift with the regulation + perspective work.

## 7 · FREQUENCY
~3 minutes (16 items, short-form base).

## 8 · ADAPTATION CHALLENGES NOTED
- **Per-subscale frames are the whole point** — the build must not collapse IRI into one "empathy score." Each subscale renders in its own frame.
- Personal Distress must never read as "you care too much" or "you don't care right" — it's a regulation/footing reading, routed to Settle.
- PT ↔ mind-reading chip cross-reference (inverses) — build them to point at each other.
- Cognitive (PT/FS) vs affective (EC/PD) split is real; keep the four distinct, don't average.
